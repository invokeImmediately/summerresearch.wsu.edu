Steps to Success

  1. Work with a mentor to develop and implement a summer research project.
  2. Complete as much of the research as possible in a timely fashion.
  3. Attend the abstract writing workshop.
  4. Submit your abstract online. ABSTRACT SUBMISSION DEADLINE: July 13, 2017
  5. Attend the poster design workshop. Download our PowerPoint poster template!
  6. Find out how your mentor wants to have your poster printed and how your mentor will pay for the cost of printing.
  7. Design a poster to be printed at a print shop, such as the College of Vetmed's Biomedical Communications Unit.
  8. Submit your poster to the print shop you and your mentor have chosen before the print shop's deadline.
  9. Pick up poster no later than the day before the event.
  10. Get dressed up and come ready to talk about your research to experts in your field as well as the general public.

Need Help?

For more information about the poster session, or to learn more about how to complete an abstract or poster design, please send an email to UGResearch@wsu.edu.

The poster symposium is the capstone experience that showcases student researchers' summer work at WSU. It also offers opportunities to talk to fellow researchers and their guests about the wide variety of work that has been completed over the past 10 weeks. The event is open to the public.

This year, the summer symposium will be from 10 a.m. to 1 p.m. on Friday, August 4 in the Smith Center for Undergraduate Education (CUE) Atrium (second floor). Light refreshments will be served at the event, and lunch will be provided for all student researchers.  Here's the symposium schedule:

  • 8:15 a.m.:  Students hang posters and participate in a photo session(s). Students should arrive no later than 8:15 a.m. to hang their posters on assigned display-board spaces; groups will be called for photos
  • 9:00-9:45 a.m. (in nearby CUE 202): Symposium welcoming remarks, featuring keynote speaker Mary Rezac, Ph.D., incoming Dean of the WSU College of Engineering and Architecture
  • 10 a.m. - 1 p.m.*: Summer Undergraduate Research Symposium is open to the public
  • 1:00 p.m.: Posters may be removed
  • *11:30 a.m. (in nearby CUE 303): Lunch for presenters only
