Steps to Success

  1. Work with a mentor to develop and implement a summer research project.
  2. Complete as much of the research as possible in a timely fashion.
  3. Attend the abstract writing workshop. (July 6)
  4. Submit your abstract online. ABSTRACT SUBMISSION DEADLINE: July 13, 2016
  5. Attend the poster design workshop. (July 20) Poster templates available!
  6. Design a poster to be printed at Large Format Printing.
  7. Submit your poster to Large Format Printing before their print deadline. POSTER PRINTING SUBMISSION DEADLINE: July 25, 2016
  8. Pick up poster the day before the event (August 1).
  9. Get dressed up and come ready to talk about your research to experts in your field as well as the general public.

Need Help?

For more information about the poster session, or to learn more about how to complete an abstract or poster design, please send an email to UGResearch@wsu.edu.

The poster symposium is the capstone experience that showcases student researchers' summer work at WSU. It also offers opportunities to talk to fellow researchers and their guests about the wide variety of work that has been completed over the past 10 weeks. The event is open to the public.

This year, the summer symposium will be from 10 a.m. to 1 p.m. on Tuesday, August 2 in the Smith Center for Undergraduate Education (CUE) Atrium (second floor). Light refreshments will be served at the event, and lunch will be provided for all student researchers.

Poster Costs and Templates

Your summer research program may be able to pay for your poster if you submit it by the deadline to the Large Format Print (LFP) Shop in the College of Engineering and Architecture. LFP is located in ETRL 203; someone is usually there from 8:30 a.m. - Noon, and 1-4 p.m. http://lfp.mme.wsu.edu/

Please talk to your program director about obtaining an Interdepartmental Requisition Invoice (IRI) to pay for the poster printing.
